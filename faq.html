Frequently Asked Questions

At Caro Chiropractic, we understand that many patients may be new to chiropractic care. Here are answers to some common questions to help you feel informed and comfortable before your visit.

What is Chiropractic? +
Chiropractic is a healthcare profession focused on the health of the nervous system and spine. We use manual adjustments to restore proper alignment and joint function, helping your body heal itself naturally without drugs or surgery.
Does the adjustment hurt? +
Generally, no! Most patients find adjustments very relaxing and feel immediate relief. You might feel a brief "stretch" sensation, and some patients experience mild muscle soreness the next day—similar to how you’d feel after a good workout.
What is that "popping" or "cracking" sound? +
It’s not your bones! That sound is called a cavitation. It is simply the release of tiny gas bubbles (oxygen, nitrogen, and CO2) from the fluid surrounding your joints when the joint space is opened.
Is chiropractic care safe? +
Yes. Chiropractic is widely recognized as one of the safest drug-free, non-invasive therapies available for back and neck pain. We perform a thorough exam before any treatment to ensure our techniques are appropriate for your specific condition.
Is it safe for children or the elderly? +
Absolutely. We modify our techniques for every age group. For seniors, we use very gentle, low-force methods to protect bone density. For children, the pressure used is often no more than you would use to check the ripeness of a tomato.
What happens during my first appointment? +
We start with a conversation about your health history and goals, followed by a thorough physical exam to check your posture, mobility, spine, muscle and nerve function. If chiropractic is right for you, we’ll discuss a personalized care plan. Many patients receive their first adjustment on this visit.
How long do appointments take? +
Your initial consultation usually takes 45 minutes - 1 hour so we can be thorough. Follow-up "regular" visits are much quicker, usually 15 to 20 minutes—designed to get you back to your day efficiently.
Do you take X-rays on-site? +
We do not take X-rays on-site, but we will refer you to a local imaging center if we determine they are clinically necessary for your safety.
How many sessions will I need? +
Every body is different. Some patients feel better in 1–2 visits, while chronic issues may take longer to stabilize. We’ll provide a recommended schedule after your first visit, focusing on getting you out of pain first, then moving toward long-term wellness.
What should I wear to my appointment? +
Loose-fitting clothing or athletic wear is best. Try to avoid stiff jeans, dresses, or bulky layers, as these can make it harder to assess your movement.
